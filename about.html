GENERAL DIRECTIVES


Student or GC students, as referred to in this manual , are those who are officially enrolled in any Gradeuate, Baccalureate, or vocational courses which are regularly offered by the College. Those enrolled in special crash courses shall be covered by this Manual except in the wearing of school uniform.

Students are expected to act as responsible citizens who respect to proper authority and fellow students while they are in the campus.

Studets must familiarize themselves with the contents of this manual and strictly comply with all the provisions stated herein.

School administrators and faculty members shall exercise authority over students and shall act as students' parents who must be responsible for correcting misbehaviors in and off the campus. All school personnel, even fellow students, posses the right and duty to report to the Office of Student Welfare and Services violation of the College rules and regulations committed in their presence.

V.ADMISSION TO THE COLLEGE

Admission to Gordon College shall be open to Filipino citizens, both residents and non-residents of Olongapo City, Non-Filipino citizens, high school graduates, or passed the high school equivalency certificate (PEPT), and shows evidence of being able to benefit from instruction. A non-resident is defined as any individual who has lived less than one year in Olongapo City, is not registered to vote in Olongapo City, and/or does no pay property taxes or contributes to the econoomic development of Olongapo City.

Admission Requirements

    Freshmen


  1. Passed Gordon College Admission Test (GCAT)

  2. One 2x2 picture

  3. Original copy of High School Report Card

  4. Photocopy of Birth Certificate

  5. Medical Certificate

  6. Barangay Clearance
  7. Photocopy of parent's/guardian's Income Tax Return (ITR) or proof of Income Photocopy of parent's/guardian's and/or the applicant's voter's registration or ID card.

  2. One 2x2 picture

  3. Original Transcript of Records

  5. Photocopy of Birth Certificate

  6. Medical Certificate

  7. Barangay Clearance

  8. Photocopy of parent's/guardian's Income Tax Return (ITR) or proof of Income
  9. Photocopy of parent's/guardian's and/or the applicant's voter's registration or ID card.



Student who intend to enrol in the vacational/short term courses shall not be required to take the Admission Test.

Returning Students
